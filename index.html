Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Will?
A Will (Last Will and Testament) is the legal document that sets out your instructions for what happens to your estate when you're no longer here. In Israel, a valid will must meet specific requirements under the Inheritance Law, 5725-1965.
What happens if I die without a Will in Israel?
If you die without a will (intestate) in Israel, your estate is distributed according to the Inheritance Law, 5725-1965 — not according to your wishes. The courts appoint an administrator and assets are divided under fixed legal rules, regardless of your personal intentions.
Do I need a lawyer to make my Will in Israel?
There is no legal requirement to use a lawyer to prepare a will in Israel. However, legal advice is strongly recommended if you have a complex estate, a second marriage, minor children, or assets outside Israel. If your situation is straightforward, our online service creates a valid, legally-binding will efficiently and affordably.
What information do I need before I start?
Very little! You'll need the names of your beneficiaries, a general sense of your assets (property, bank accounts, investments), and decisions about who you wish to appoint as executor. You don't need exact financial figures to get started.
Is an online will legally valid in Israel?
Yes, provided it is signed in the presence of two witnesses who are not beneficiaries. Our service includes clear, step-by-step signing instructions to ensure your will meets all Israeli legal requirements.
